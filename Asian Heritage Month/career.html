Career & Awards

Sandra Oh's most recognisable works include being part of Grey's Anatomy seasons 1-10 acting as Christina Yang, as well as playing Eve Polastri in Killing Eve. Though beyond these works Oh has been featured in movies, tv shows, and even voice acting for over 30 years, being involved from a very early age. One of her first roles was portraying Evelyn Lau in the CBC movie The Diary of Evelyn Lau from 1993. This was one of her very first roles in more mainstream media at the age of 22.

Oh moved to Hollywood in 1996 to expand her horizons and give herself more oppourtunities. One of which was a sit-com called Arli$$ where she played a charcter named Rita Wu on and off for 6 years. Throughout this time Oh also made appearances in other films, this including award-winning Canadian films, as well as big box office films that are still talked about today. Those including the supporting roles she played in The Princess Diaries, the Walking Dead, and Big Fat liar.

One of her following roles 3 years later as Dr. Christina Yang on ABC's Grey's Anatomy became a cult favourite. Oh played this role for 9 years and finally chosing to part with the series in the 10th season. Throughout these 9 years she also made time to voice act for animated shows such as American Dad! or Phineas and Ferb. Grey's Anatomy becoming so mainstream and popular allowed for Oh's career to take off and opened her to many more opportunities. In 2016 after she had left the show already she voice-acted and helped produce the animatation Window Horses. One of her more recent works has been staring as Eve Polastri in the BBC tv show Killing Eve. Her extraordinary perforamnce in Killing Eve also got her nominated for many awards.

Throughout her career as an actress and producer Sandra Oh has accumulated a mass amount of awards. Of the most notable she received 2 Golden Globe Awards, being the first Asian woman to do so. The first one was received in 2005 for her work in Grey's Anatomy as the best supporting actress in a series, miniseries, or television film. And the second one was from 2019 as the best actress in a television series drama for her role in Killing Eve. Not only has Oh received Golden Globe Awards, but she hosted the 76th Golden Globe Awards, being the first Asian to very do so as well. Oh has also received 12 Primetime Emmy Award Nominations and winning four Screen Actors Guild Awards. In the 2019 Time's Magazine list for the top 100 most influentional people Sandra Oh was amongst it as well.
